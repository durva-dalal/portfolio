01 — Subko: Beyond the Brew MA Dissertation · Creative Direction · 2024
The Brief

An Indian coffee brand wants into the UK

Subko is a specialty coffee roastery founded in Mumbai in 2020. Known for authenticity and rapid growth in India, the brand wanted to penetrate the UK market. The challenge: the UK coffee market is worth £11.3 billion, dominated by Costa, Starbucks, Greggs, and Caffe Nero. India is the 8th largest coffee producer globally, yet has almost zero international brand recognition.

The Research

Understand before you create

PESTEL analysis, full competitor mapping across the top five UK coffee chains, a category map plotting accessibility against sustainability, and a semiotic analysis of how existing brands communicate. Key finding: the gap is high sustainability combined with high accessibility. Every sustainable brand is niche. Every accessible brand has weak sustainability credentials. Subko's opportunity was to own that intersection.

Three Creative Routes Explored

Before deciding on direction

Concept 01

"Brewed in Tradition"

Heritage-led. The journey of Indian coffee beans from plantation to cup, told through storytelling and experiential campus pop-ups.

Concept 02

"Sustainability on the Go"

Eco-first. Sustainable sourcing, fair trade, eco-conscious packaging made accessible and affordable for students.

Final — "Beyond the Brew"

Desi Brew by Subko

A sub-brand for the UK university market. Custom logotype blending Latin and Devanagari scripts. Earthy palette. Indian folk-art patterns. Packaging, social, campus launch executions, a collapsible reusable mug, and seed-embedded takeaway cups. Slogan: "Soil to Soul."

02 — O2 Telefonica: Weather Hijack MA Module · Campaign Strategy · 2024
The Brief

Get O2 noticed by people who scroll past everything

Pick a telecoms brand, identify a real problem it faces, develop a creative campaign solution using at least three media channels. I chose O2. The problem was specific: O2's existing marketing was successfully reaching older demographics but actively alienating 16 to 30 year olds. Raise awareness without producing another forgettable telecoms ad.

The Strategic Insight

Weather is the only thing everyone shares in real time

Young people tune out static advertising. They engage with things that feel relevant to the exact moment they are in. Weather is the most universally shared real-time experience. Everyone in a city experiences the same weather simultaneously. Perfect vehicle for a brand whose platform is "Together, Everywhere."

The Campaign: Three Channels, One Idea

Together, Everywhere

Channel 01 — Primary

Weather-Reactive Digital OOH

Digital billboards in high-footfall urban areas and near university campuses. Powered by live weather API data. Copy changes automatically based on temperature, precipitation, and conditions. Always current. Always relevant.

Channel 02 — Extension

Social Media Mirror

Time-stamped social posts that mirror the exact billboard copy for the conditions at time of posting. The brand is present in the same moment as the audience, not broadcasting at them.

Channel 03 — Supporting

Weather Hijack

Secondary executions inside contexts users already pay attention to: weather app interfaces, transport screens, commuter environments. All copy written by me.
