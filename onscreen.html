TV Commercial · 10 Films Gamezy — IPL Campaign Series 2022 and 2023 +
The Brief

India's IPL season. A fantasy sports market with six loud players. How do you get noticed?

Gamezy, a fast-growing fantasy cricket platform, came in with a specific challenge: the IPL season is the noisiest sporting moment in India, and every competitor was running near-identical messaging around winning big. The brief was to cut through — not by being louder, but by being sharper. Two seasons. Ten commercials. The work needed to be funny, fast, and rooted in something users genuinely felt.

My Role

Lead scriptwriter across both seasons. Responsible for the campaign concept, all ten scripts, and the overarching narrative line. Worked directly with the production team to ensure the scripts translated on screen — not just on paper.

The Insight That Drove Everything

"App Badlo" — Change Your App

The sharpest insight on the project: when fantasy cricket players lose, they blame the app, not themselves. That is not a flaw in user psychology — it is a door. "App Badlo" gave frustrated Gamezy rivals a funny, self-aware way out. It did not argue that Gamezy was better. It acknowledged the frustration, gave it a name, and handed the user a solution. Comedy as strategy.

Each film featured a different losing scenario — bad picks, wrong captains, last-minute substitutions — all ending the same way: switch the app. The consistency of the idea across ten films made it a campaign, not just a collection of spots.

📊 Avg 2M+ views per film. Broadcast nationally during IPL across two consecutive seasons. Campaign became recognisable enough that the "App Badlo" line entered the conversation among fantasy cricket communities.
Digital Campaign · 2 Films Kushal's Jewellery — Rakshabandhan 2022 +
The Brief

India's biggest gifting festival. One jewellery brand. Make it feel personal.

Rakshabandhan is one of India's biggest sibling festivals — brothers gift sisters, and the pressure to get it right is real. Kushal's, a national jewellery chain, wanted a campaign that would drive awareness and footfall during the peak gifting window. The brief: make the brand feel less transactional and more emotionally relevant to younger audiences.

The Insight and My Contribution

The strategy leaned into a universal truth: brothers are notoriously bad at buying jewellery for sisters. Not because they do not care — because every sister is different, and the thought of getting it wrong is paralysing. The campaign reframed Kushal's not as a jewellery store but as the answer to that exact anxiety. Script, campaign concept, and narrative direction. Two films, each telling the story through a different sister archetype.

Digital Campaign ICICI Bank — Father's Day 2022 +
The Brief and Approach

A banking ad that does not feel like a banking ad

ICICI Bank's brief was to connect their youth banking account to the emotional bond between a father and child — timed to Father's Day. The challenge: banking ads that lead with emotion almost always tip into sentimentality and lose credibility. The approach was comedy-emotional. Show the absurd lengths a father will go through for his child — with warmth, with laughter — and let the landing on ICICI Bank feel earned rather than bolted on. Script and creative direction. The film aired on Instagram and received strong organic engagement for a financial product ad.

Digital Campaign · 2 Films ICICI iMobile Pay — Wes Anderson Series 2022 +
The Brief and Approach

Two product feature ads that feel like films you actually want to watch

The brief was to explain features of ICICI's iMobile Pay app — a payment and banking application — without producing a product tutorial. The problem with fintech advertising is that features are inherently dry. The solution was to wrap each feature in a distinct visual language that did all the heavy lifting before the message landed. Two Wes Anderson-style short films: symmetrical framing, deadpan delivery, a visual grammar so considered that viewers engaged with the aesthetic before they registered the banking message. The format earned attention; the product information landed in that earned space. Script and creative direction for both films.

Brand Film Netbrahma — Flexible Working 2022 +
The Brief and Insight

A company that gets creatives needs a film that proves it — not just says it

Netbrahma is a platform that gives creative professionals the freedom to work on their own terms. The brief was to produce a brand film that communicated this proposition — but the constraint was that the audience are creatives themselves. They will see through anything that feels like a corporate marketing claim. The film had to demonstrate the brand's personality, not describe it. Playful, self-aware, and structurally a little odd in the way that genuinely creative things tend to be. Script and creative concept.

NGO and Purpose Films — Case Files
CSR Film · ICICI Foundation Sariska Tiger Reserve — ST9's Story 2024 +
The Brief

ICICI Foundation's conservation work spans 56 forests. Tell it through one tiger.

ICICI Foundation's CSR programme spans 56 forests and tiger reserves across India — water conservation, grassland augmentation, enhanced ranger resources, livelihood support for border communities. The brief: communicate the scale and depth of that impact without producing a corporate impact report. A film that felt like it belonged on a nature channel, not in a boardroom presentation.

My Role and Approach

Script conceptualisation and writing, casting, costume direction, and post-production edits. The storytelling choice was to tell the entire story through the perspective of ST9 — a female tiger at Sariska Tiger Reserve. By anchoring the narrative in one animal's thriving existence, the film made the conservation work feel immediate and personal rather than institutional. Shot across difficult terrain over multiple days. The challenge was holding a single emotional throughline across a complex environmental brief with many stakeholders.

🌿 Film used by ICICI Foundation across internal and external communications. Also cut into short-form Instagram reels for social distribution. Demonstrated conservation impact at scale through individual storytelling.
Global NGO Film · Gates Foundation Together Against TB — Chandraprakash's Story 2023 +
The Brief

TB is curable. The brief was not about the disease. It was about what surrounds it.

Produced in collaboration with the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation for their India TB-free initiative. The brief was not a clinical one — it was a social one. Stigma, isolation, and the absence of community support are as damaging to TB outcomes as the disease itself. The task was to show what collective care looks like in practice, without the film feeling like a public health poster.

My Role and the Craft Involved

Script conceptualisation and writing, casting, costume direction, and post-production edits. The film tells the story of Chandraprakash and the community of Khushipur that refused to let him face his diagnosis alone. Writing for a global health organisation requires balancing emotional storytelling with factual accuracy, and absolute cultural sensitivity — the story had to be true to its community, not a Western interpretation of rural India.

Corporate Film Save the Children — Corporate Film 2022 +
The Brief and My Contribution

One of the world's largest children's rights organisations. A film that earns trust rather than demanding it.

Corporate film scripted for Save the Children, one of the world's most recognised children's rights organisations with operations across 100+ countries. The challenge with NGO corporate films is avoiding the trap of becoming a charity appeal. The brief required emotional clarity without emotional manipulation. Trust over persuasion. The film needed to communicate credibility and compassion simultaneously — two things that often pull in opposite directions in charity communications. Script, casting, costume direction, and post-production edits. The final film was used for internal stakeholder and partner communications.
